## THE PATTERN

Both Gap and Glean interviews followed the same core structure, but with different emphasis based on the role:

### Gap (Sr. Manager, AI Strategy & Enterprise Enablement)
**Role Type**: Internal transformation strategy (enabler role)  
**Focus**: Enterprise-wide governance, adoption frameworks, strategic positioning  
**Proof Points**: AWS enablement (+17% attendance, +50% high-CSAT), Palette architecture

### Glean (AI Outcomes Manager)
**Role Type**: External customer outcomes delivery (executor role)  
**Focus**: Hands-on agent design, customer engagement, measurable outcomes  
**Proof Points**: Palette production system (7 agents, 3 active projects), AWS GenAI partnerships

## THE 5-PHASE PROCESS

### Phase 1: Foundation & Positioning (30 minutes)
**What**: Define your core narrative and competitive advantages

**Gap Approach**:
- Positioned as "enabler, not executor"
- Domain-agnostic methodology (not retail expertise)
- AWS anchor story (enablement behavior change)
- Two AI benefits framework (impossible without AI + employee experience)

**Glean Approach**:
- Positioned as "builder and executor"
- Palette as proof of shipped outcomes (not demos)
- AWS GenAI partnerships (LLM expertise)
- 4-Question Method framework (pattern, data, repetitive, measurable)

**Key Insight**: Tailor positioning to role type (enabler vs. executor)

---

### Phase 2: Deep Research (1-2 hours)
**What**: Understand the company, product, market, and role

**Gap Approach** (Argy-led research):
- ROI models and financial framing
- Risk/governance frameworks
- Operating model research (FDE model, LVMH governance)
- Retail landscape analysis
- Current state research (Gap's ~200 AI models, tool sprawl)

**Glean Approach** (Streamlined research):
- Company overview (founding, mission, product evolution)
- Product architecture (search, assistant, agent builder)
- Funding & valuation ($360M+, $2.2B valuation)
- Market position (competitors, differentiation)
- Customer insights (use cases, success metrics, objections)

**Key Insight**: Gap required more research (unfamiliar domain), Glean required less (familiar domain)

---

### Phase 3: Strategic Frameworks (1-2 hours)
**What**: Build frameworks that demonstrate your thinking

**Gap Approach** (Rex-led frameworks):
- 5 big ideas (FDE model, LVMH governance, operations-first, behavior change, budget shrinks)
- Use case deep dives (inventory forecasting, store associate assistant, markdown optimization)
- Adoption framework (champions, 5-minute recipes, persona-specific UX)
- Executive narrative (seams theory, staffing inversion, new data streams)

**Glean Approach** (Simplified frameworks):
- 4-Question Method (pattern, data, repetitive, measurable)
- Agent workflow design (input â†’ retrieval â†’ generation â†’ human-in-the-loop)
- Pilot scoping (12 weeks, 5-10 users, success metrics, kill criteria)
- Success metrics (usage, efficiency, business outcomes)

**Key Insight**: Gap required strategic frameworks (enterprise transformation), Glean required tactical frameworks (customer engagement)

---

### Phase 4: Validation (30 minutes)
**What**: Check for coherence, accuracy, and completeness

**Gap Approach** (Anky-led validation):
- Architecture audit (is the plan coherent?)
- Memory validation (are all artifacts aligned?)
- Coherence check (does the narrative hold together?)

**Glean Approach** (Self-validation):
- Does the opening statement address all "must-haves"?
- Does the demo scenario show customer empathy?
- Are the proof points credible and verifiable?

**Key Insight**: Gap required formal validation (complex plan), Glean required informal validation (simpler plan)

---

### Phase 5: Interview Prep (1-2 hours)
**What**: Create interview materials (cheat sheet, Q&A, demo script)

**Gap Approach**:
- Interview cheat sheet (11.6KB)
- Answer library for 10 question categories
- Out-of-the-box differentiators (seams theory, staffing inversion, new data streams)

**Glean Approach**:

## THE REUSABLE TEMPLATE

### Step 1: Foundation (30 minutes)
1. Read job description carefully
2. Identify role type (enabler vs. executor, internal vs. external)
3. Define your core narrative (how do your proof points map to this role?)
4. Identify competitive advantages (what makes you different?)

### Step 2: Research (1-2 hours)
1. Company overview (founding, mission, product evolution)
2. Product/service details (what do they sell? who are their customers?)
3. Market position (competitors, differentiation, funding/valuation)
4. Customer insights (use cases, success metrics, common objections)
5. Role details (what they're looking for, compensation, team structure)

### Step 3: Frameworks (1-2 hours)
1. Identify 2-3 frameworks that demonstrate your thinking
2. Build demo scenario (how would you work with this company/customer?)
3. Create success metrics (how would you measure outcomes?)
4. Design pilot/project scope (12 weeks, clear success criteria, kill criteria)

### Step 4: Validation (30 minutes)
1. Does your opening statement address all "must-haves" in job description?
2. Does your demo scenario show relevant skills and experience?
3. Are your proof points credible and verifiable?
4. Does your narrative hold together (no contradictions or gaps)?

### Step 5: Interview Prep (1-2 hours)
1. Create cheat sheet (quick reference for interview day)
2. Write opening statement (2-min and 30-sec versions)
3. Build Q&A library (top 10 questions with answers)
4. Prepare demo script (if relevant)
5. List questions to ask (5-10 intelligent questions)
6. Rehearse out loud (opening statement 3X, demo scenario 2X, top 5 questions 1X each)

**Total Time**: 4-6 hours (can be done in one day or spread over 2-3 days)

## KEY LESSONS LEARNED

### 1. Tailor Positioning to Role Type
- **Enabler roles** (Gap): Emphasize strategic thinking, governance, adoption frameworks
- **Executor roles** (Glean): Emphasize hands-on building, customer engagement, measurable outcomes

### 2. Research Depth Depends on Domain Familiarity
- **Unfamiliar domain** (Gap retail): Deep research required (8+ documents)
- **Familiar domain** (Glean AI/SaaS): Streamlined research sufficient (5 documents)

### 3. Frameworks Should Match Role Complexity
- **Strategic roles** (Gap): Complex frameworks (enterprise transformation, governance, ROI)
- **Tactical roles** (Glean): Simple frameworks (customer engagement, agent design, pilot scoping)

### 4. Proof Points Are Reusable
- Palette, AWS GenAI, AWS Public Sector work for both roles
- Emphasis changes (Palette architecture for Gap, Palette outcomes for Glean)
- Same proof points, different framing

### 5. Demo Scenarios Are Critical
- **Gap**: Show how you'd enable enterprise transformation (leadership alignment, pilot design, adoption framework)
- **Glean**: Show how you'd work with a customer (discovery, agent design, pilot scoping, outcome measurement)

### 6. Materials Should Be Modular
- **Glean approach is better**: 5 separate documents (package, research, cheat sheet, demo, README)
- **Gap approach was monolithic**: 1-2 large documents
- Modular is easier to navigate and rehearse from

## FINAL THOUGHTS

**What worked for Gap**:
- Deep research (8+ documents) because retail was unfamiliar
- Strategic frameworks (enterprise transformation, governance, ROI)
- Out-of-the-box differentiators (seams theory, staffing inversion, new data streams)

**What worked for Glean**:
- Streamlined research (5 documents) because AI/SaaS was familiar
- Tactical frameworks (customer engagement, agent design, pilot scoping)
- Modular materials (easier to navigate and rehearse from)

**What works for both**:
- Lead with proof points (Palette, AWS GenAI, AWS Public Sector)
- Build demo scenario (show how you'd work with customer/company)
- Create success metrics (show how you'd measure outcomes)
- Rehearse out loud (opening 3X, demo 2X, top 5 questions 1X each)

**The key**: Tailor positioning and frameworks to role type, but keep proof points and process consistent.
