## Overview: Integrated Three-Pillar System

The three expansion pillars (community events, art workshops, charitable artist residencies) are not separate programs - they are an **integrated artist career pipeline**. This is Rossi's core innovation: systematizing the proven trajectory of successful graffiti artists (Chito, Barry McGee) and making it accessible to 80+ artists simultaneously.

**The Formula**: Signature style + personal champion + documentation = career launch [Research: task-5-comparable-organizations.md, Chito trajectory]

**The Pipeline**: Workshops discover talent and develop signature styles → Residencies provide champions and documentation → Events showcase work → Artists launch careers with gallery representation and brand collaborations.

## Stage 1: Discovery & Signature Style Development (Workshops)

### Purpose
Teach emerging artists to develop distinctive signature style - the prerequisite for career launch.

### Workshop Structure

**Public Workshops** (Monthly, 12/year):
- **Duration**: 4 hours per session, 3-month series (Fundamentals → Style Development → Business Skills)
- **Capacity**: 12-15 people per workshop
- **Pricing**: Sliding scale $50-$150 (community rate $50, standard $100, supporter $150)
- **Location**: SF flagship Year 1, partner locations Year 2-3

**Corporate Workshops** (Quarterly, 4-6/year):
- **Duration**: 3-4 hours per session
- **Capacity**: 15-25 people (team-building)
- **Pricing**: $2,500-$5,000 per session
- **Purpose**: Revenue generator (subsidizes community workshops)

**School Programs** (Grant-Funded, 10-15/year):
- **Duration**: 2-4 sessions per school
- **Capacity**: 20-30 students per session
- **Pricing**: Free to schools (funded by SF Arts Commission, California Arts Council grants)
- **Purpose**: Community impact, discover young talent

### Curriculum

**Month 1: Fundamentals**
- Spray technique (can control, pressure, distance)
- Color theory (mixing, layering, contrast)
- Composition (balance, focal points, negative space)
- Safety & legality (proper ventilation, legal walls, permits)

**Month 2: Style Development**
- Finding your voice (what makes your work distinctive?)
- Building visual language (recurring motifs, color palettes, techniques)
- Studying influences (Mission School, graffiti history, contemporary artists)
- Iteration & refinement (create 10+ pieces, identify patterns)

**Month 3: Business Skills**
- Pricing work (how to value originals, prints, commissions)
- Contracts & IP rights (protect your work, negotiate deals)
- Gallery relationships (how to approach galleries, what to expect)
- Career planning (set goals, track progress, build network)

### Instructors

**Teaching Artists** (5-8 from Rossi roster):
- Working graffiti artists (not academic instructors)
- Paid 60% of workshop revenue ($48K/year Year 1, $90K/year Year 2, $150K/year Year 3 distributed among 5-8 artists)
- Trained in curriculum delivery (consistency across instructors)

### Success Metrics

**Conversion Rate**: 10-15% of workshop participants join Rossi roster (trial period)
- Year 1: 144 participants → 14-22 new roster artists
- Year 2: 240 participants → 24-36 new roster artists
- Year 3: 360 participants → 36-54 new roster artists

**Community Impact**: 500+ Mission District residents served annually through sliding-scale pricing

**Revenue**: $80K Year 1 → $150K Year 2 → $250K Year 3

## Stage 2: Champion & Network Building (Residencies)

### Purpose
Provide personal champion (mentor) and network connections - the catalyst for career launch.

### Residency Structure

**Duration**: 1-2 months (artist choice)

**Stipend**: 
- 1-month: $3K stipend + $1K materials
- 2-month: $6K stipend + $2K materials

**Studio Space**: Dedicated workspace at Rossi flagship or partner location

**Mentor Assignment**: Established Rossi artist mentors resident (paid $500-$1K mentorship fee)

**Network Access**: Introductions to collectors, galleries, brands (mentor facilitates)

**Exhibition**: Residency culminates in solo or group show (opening night, press, sales)

**Documentation**: Content team captures entire process (artist story video, process time-lapses, Instagram takeover)

### Sponsorship Model

**Sponsorship Levels**:
- $5,000: 1-month residency (artist stipend $3K, materials $1K, documentation $1K)
- $10,000: 2-month residency (artist stipend $6K, materials $2K, documentation $2K)
- $25,000: Named residency (artist stipend $15K, materials $5K, documentation $5K, sponsor recognition)

**Sponsor Benefits**:
- Recognition (named residency, exhibition signage, press mentions)
- Collector access (private preview, first purchase rights)
- Community impact (support emerging artist career)
- Tax deduction (501(c)(3) donation)

**Target Sponsors**:
- Tech companies (Google, Salesforce, Airbnb, startups) - cultural investment
- Individual collectors (existing Rossi collectors) - discover artists early
- Foundations (SF Arts Commission, California Arts Council, private foundations)

### Selection Process

**Application**: Open call (quarterly), portfolio + artist statement + career goals

**Review**: Artist advisory council evaluates (artistic quality, career readiness, cultural fit)

**Selection**: 6-10 residents per year (Year 1), 20-30 per year (Year 3 network-wide)

### Success Metrics

**Career Launch Rate**: 80% of residents secure gallery representation or brand collaboration within 12 months

**Social Media Growth**: Artist following grows 50-200% during residency (documentation effect)

**Revenue**: $30K Year 1 → $50K Year 2 → $100K Year 3

## Stage 3: Documentation & Visibility (Content Production)

### Purpose
Create documentation that "travels globally within hours" [Research: task-1-global-markets.md] - the discovery engine that drives collector demand.

### Content Types

**Artist Stories** (5-10 min video profiles):
- Signature style (what makes their work distinctive)
- Process (how they create, tools, techniques)
- Inspiration (Mission District, graffiti history, personal story)
- Career trajectory (where they've been, where they're going)
- **Production**: 2-3 per month (24-36/year Year 1, 240-540/year Year 3 network-wide)

**Process Videos** (Time-lapse, 1-3 min):
- Mural creation (street to completion)
- Studio work (canvas, mixed media)
- Live painting (events, exhibitions)
- **Production**: 1-2 per month (12-24/year)

**Workshop Recaps** (Highlight reels, 2-3 min):
- Student work (before/after)
- Instructor teaching (technique demonstrations)
- Testimonials (student feedback)
- **Production**: Monthly (12/year)

**Event Coverage** (Recap videos, 3-5 min):
- Exhibition openings (crowd, artist, sales)
- Live painting events (process, audience reaction)
- Music/art crossovers (performances, collaborations)
- **Production**: Monthly (12/year)

**Behind-the-Scenes** (Instagram stories, reels, 15-60 sec):
- Artist interviews (quick Q&A)
- Studio visits (workspace tours)
- Residency updates (progress check-ins)
- **Production**: Weekly (52/year)

### Distribution Channels

**Instagram**: Daily posts, stories, reels (primary discovery channel)
**YouTube**: Long-form artist stories, process videos (SEO, evergreen content)
**TikTok**: Short-form process videos, workshop highlights (viral potential)
**Email Newsletter**: Monthly, artist spotlights + event calendar (collector engagement)
**Website**: Artist profiles, portfolio galleries, shop (conversion)

### Production Team

**Content Producer** (1.0 FTE Year 1, 1.5 FTE Year 3):
- Shoot, edit, publish content
- Manage social media channels
- Coordinate with artists (schedule shoots, get approvals)

**Equipment**: Camera, lenses, lighting, audio, editing software ($10K-$15K initial investment)

**Budget**: $60K-$100K/year (Content Producer salary + equipment + editing)

### Success Metrics

**Social Media Growth**:
- Instagram: 5K followers Year 1 → 25K Year 3
- YouTube: 1K subscribers Year 1 → 10K Year 3
- TikTok: 10K followers Year 1 → 100K Year 3

**Website Traffic**: 10K visits/month Year 1 → 50K visits/month Year 3

**Content ROI**: Track sales attributed to content (artist discovered via Instagram, YouTube, etc.)

## Integration: How the Three Stages Work Together

### Example: Emerging Artist "Maria"

**Month 1-3: Workshop (Discovery)**
- Maria takes 3-month workshop series
- Develops signature style: geometric graffiti + Mission District iconography
- Instructor identifies Maria as high-potential (recommends for roster trial)

**Month 4-6: Roster Trial (Validation)**
- Maria joins Rossi roster (trial period)
- Exhibits 10 pieces at SF flagship
- Sells 5 pieces ($2,500 revenue, $1,250 to Maria)
- Content team creates artist story video (Instagram, YouTube)

**Month 7-9: Residency (Champion)**
- Maria selected for 2-month residency (corporate sponsor, $10K)
- Mentored by established Rossi artist "Carlos"
- Carlos introduces Maria to collectors, galleries, brand contacts
- Maria creates 10 new pieces during residency
- Content team documents entire process (artist story, process time-lapses, Instagram takeover)

**Month 10: Exhibition (Launch)**
- Maria's residency culminates in solo show at SF flagship
- Opening night: 150 attendees, 8 pieces sold ($12K revenue, $6K to Maria)
- Content: Exhibition coverage video, collector interviews, social media amplification
- Press: SF Chronicle covers show
- Social: Maria's Instagram grows from 500 to 3,000 followers

**Month 11-12: Career Launch (Outcome)**
- Maria secures gallery representation in LA (Rossi partner)
- Lands brand collaboration (local SF business, $5K commission)
- Invited to paint mural for "Vacant to Vibrant" program
- Total Year 1 earnings: $12K+ (Rossi sales + brand collaboration + mural commission)

**Result**: Maria goes from unknown workshop participant to represented artist with brand deals in 12 months.

**This is the Rossi model**: Systematize what happened organically for Chito and Barry McGee, make it accessible to 80+ artists.
