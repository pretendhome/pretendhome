## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Mission**: Make 10,000+ Gap Inc. employees comfortable with AI — not afraid. Help them understand how incredible it is to work in conjunction with AI, so they feel more valued as individuals, deliver faster and more complete work, and have confidence in what they deliver.

**Approach**: Behavior change, not just training. Build 5-minute "recipes" (live workflows employees can copy immediately). Measure time-to-decision improvement and confidence scores, not just login counts.

**Mical's AWS Proof Point**:
- +17% attendance (153 → 179 avg/session)
- +50% high-CSAT sessions (>4.9)
- +67% sales plays covered (133 → 222)
- Key: Shifted from "users aren't technical enough" to "systems aren't aligned to how people work"

**Gap Target**: Replicate AWS success across 4 brands, 10,000+ employees

## 2. ADOPTION MEASUREMENT (BEHAVIOR CHANGE METRICS)

### Leading Indicators (Predict Success)

**Engagement Metrics**:
- Daily Active Users (DAU) / Weekly Active Users (WAU) / Monthly Active Users (MAU)
- Feature usage depth (how many features used per session)
- Recipe completion rate (% of employees who complete 5-minute recipes)
- Time-to-first-value (how long until first successful use)

**Sentiment Metrics**:
- Confidence scores (self-reported: "I feel confident using AI for X")
- CSAT (satisfaction with AI tools)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS): "Would you recommend this AI tool to a colleague?"
- Feedback volume (qualitative: what's working, what's not)

**Behavior Metrics**:
- Time-to-decision (how long to complete a task: before AI vs. after AI)
- Override rate (how often employees override AI recommendations)
- Escalation rate (how often employees escalate to manager)
- Error rate (how often AI gives wrong answer, employee catches it)

---

### Lagging Indicators (Measure Impact)

**Business Outcomes**:
- Cost reduction (inventory waste, labor costs, markdown waste)
- Productivity improvement (time freed up, tasks completed)
- Quality improvement (forecast accuracy, customer satisfaction)
- Revenue impact (sales lift, margin improvement)

**Organizational Health**:
- Employee retention (turnover rate)
- Employee satisfaction (engagement surveys)
- Training completion (% of employees trained)
- Champions program growth (# of champions, # of peer trainings)

---

### LVMH Pattern: Dashboard Use as KPI

**Adoption Rule**: Dashboard use IS a KPI — non-adoption halts initiatives

**Gap Implementation**:
- Track dashboard usage per persona (DAU/WAU/MAU)
- Set thresholds: 80% DAU (store associates), 90% WAU (store managers), 95% DAU (merchandisers)
- If usage drops below threshold for 2 consecutive weeks → pause rollout, investigate, fix
- If usage stays below threshold for 4 weeks → kill initiative, learn, iterate

**Why This Works**: Forces focus on adoption (not just deployment). If employees don't use it, it doesn't matter how good the AI is.

## 3. TRAINING ARCHITECTURE (5-MINUTE RECIPES)

### Recipe Structure (Per Persona, Per Use Case)

**Recipe Template**:
1. **Problem** (30 seconds): What pain point does this solve?
2. **Solution** (1 minute): How does AI help?
3. **Demo** (2 minutes): Live workflow (screen recording or in-person)
4. **Try It** (1.5 minutes): Employee tries it themselves (guided)
5. **Next Steps** (30 seconds): Where to get help, how to give feedback

**Total**: 5 minutes (not 30-minute training, not 2-hour workshop)

---

### Example Recipe: "How to Find a Product in 30 Seconds" (Store Associates)

**Problem** (30 seconds):
"Customer asks: 'Do you have this sweater in size M?' You don't know. You walk to the back, search manually, come back 5 minutes later. Customer is gone."

**Solution** (1 minute):
"AI assistant on your phone. Ask: 'Do we have [product name] in size M?' AI checks inventory in real-time. Answers in 5 seconds: 'Yes, aisle 3, shelf 2' or 'No, but we have it online, I can order for you.'"

**Demo** (2 minutes):
[Screen recording: Associate opens app, asks question via voice, AI responds with location, associate walks to aisle, finds product, returns to customer in 30 seconds]

**Try It** (1.5 minutes):
"Now you try. Open the app. Ask: 'Do we have [product name] in size L?' See what AI says. Walk to the location. Did you find it? If yes, great. If no, report it (helps AI learn)."

**Next Steps** (30 seconds):
"Questions? Ask your store manager or text the AI team. Feedback? Use the feedback button in the app. We read every comment."

## 4. CHAMPIONS PROGRAM (PEER ADOPTION)

### Champion Selection Criteria

**Who Makes a Good Champion**:
- Early adopter (uses AI tool in pilot, gives feedback)
- Peer respected (colleagues trust them, not just management)
- Communicator (can explain AI in simple terms)
- Enthusiast (genuinely excited about AI, not forced)

**Who NOT to Select**:
- Management mandate ("You're the champion because I said so")
- Tech expert only (can't relate to non-technical peers)
- Skeptic (doesn't believe in AI, won't advocate)

---

### Champion Responsibilities

**Time Commitment**: 2-4 hours/week

**Activities**:
1. **Deliver recipes** (5 minutes each, 2-3 per week)
2. **Answer peer questions** (Slack, in-person, ad-hoc)
3. **Give feedback to FDEs** (what's working, what's not)
4. **Share tips and tricks** (peer-to-peer learning)
5. **Advocate for AI** (positive stories, success cases)

**Compensation**:
- Recognition (public acknowledgment, certificate)
- Access (early access to new features, direct line to FDEs)
- Development (leadership training, career growth opportunity)
- Optional: Small stipend ($500-$1,000/quarter)

---

### Champion Program Structure

**Pilot Phase** (Year 1):
- 10-20 champions (2-4 per pilot: Old Navy, Gap, Banana Republic)
- FDEs recruit champions during pilot (identify early adopters)
- Champions deliver recipes to next 50-100 users (scale phase)

**Scale Phase** (Year 2):
- 100-200 champions (20-40 per brand, 10-20 per corporate function)
- Champions deliver recipes to 1,000-2,000 users (enterprise scale)
- Champions form community (Slack channel, monthly meetings)

**Sustain Phase** (Year 3):
- 200-300 champions (self-sustaining community)
- Champions onboard new employees (AI training part of onboarding)
- Champions drive continuous improvement (feedback loop to FDEs)

---

### Champion Success Metrics

**Individual Champion**:
- # of recipes delivered (target: 2-3/week)
- # of peers trained (target: 10-20/quarter)
- Peer satisfaction (CSAT with champion training)

**Program Overall**:
- # of champions (target: 10-20 Year 1, 100-200 Year 2, 200-300 Year 3)

## 5. SHADOW AI PREVENTION (GOVERNANCE WITHOUT KILLING EXPERIMENTATION)

### The Problem

**Shadow AI**: Employees use unauthorized AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) outside of Gap's governance

**Why It Happens**:
- Official AI tools don't meet their needs (too slow, too limited, too complex)
- Employees don't know official tools exist (poor communication)
- Employees don't trust official tools (prefer familiar tools)

**Why It's a Problem**:
- Data leakage (employees paste sensitive data into public AI tools)
- Brand safety (employees use AI to generate customer-facing content without guardrails)
- Compliance (CCPA, GDPR, PCI violations)
- Inconsistency (different employees use different tools, no shared learning)

---

### The Solution (Governance Without Killing Experimentation)

**Principle**: Make official AI tools BETTER than shadow AI (not just "allowed")

**Tactics**:

**1. Make Official Tools Easy to Use**
- Mobile-first (accessible anywhere)
- Voice interface (hands-free)
- Fast (sub-second response time)
- Integrated (works with existing tools: Workday, ServiceNow, inventory systems)

**2. Make Official Tools More Capable**
- Access to Gap-specific data (inventory, sales, SOPs) — ChatGPT doesn't have this
- Brand-specific guardrails (prevents off-brand content) — ChatGPT doesn't have this
- Conversational memory (remembers context) — ChatGPT forgets after session

**3. Communicate Official Tools Exist**
- Onboarding (new employees learn about AI tools on Day 1)
- Recipes (5-minute demos show what's possible)
- Champions (peer advocacy, not corporate mandate)

**4. Monitor Shadow AI (Without Surveillance)**
- Network monitoring (detect ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity usage)
- Don't punish (educate: "We have an official tool that's better")
- Understand why (what need is shadow AI meeting that official tools aren't?)

**5. Iterate Based on Shadow AI Usage**
- If employees use ChatGPT for X, add X to official tools
- If employees use Claude for Y, add Y to official tools
- Shadow AI is a signal (not a problem) — it tells you what employees need

---

### Shadow AI Policy (Governance)

**Allowed**:
- Use official AI tools (Gemini Enterprise, Store Associate AI Assistant, etc.)
- Experiment with AI for personal learning (not work-related)
- Give feedback to FDEs (request new features)

**Not Allowed**:
- Paste sensitive data into public AI tools (customer PII, financial data, trade secrets)
- Use AI to generate customer-facing content without review (brand safety)
- Use AI for compliance-sensitive tasks without approval (legal, HR, finance)

**Enforcement**:
- Education first (not punishment)
- If repeated violations → escalate to manager
- If data leakage → escalate to Legal/Compliance

## 6. RETAIL-SPECIFIC UX PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Mobile-First (Store Associates)

**Why**: Store associates are on the sales floor, not at a desk

**Design**:
- Mobile app (iOS/Android)
- Voice interface (hands-free)
- Large buttons (easy to tap with gloves or in a hurry)
- Offline mode (works without Wi-Fi in store)

---

### Principle 2: Desktop + Mobile (Store Managers, Merchandisers)

**Why**: Managers and merchandisers work at desks but also need mobile access

**Design**:
- Desktop dashboard (detailed analytics, forecasts)
- Mobile app (alerts, quick actions)
- Sync across devices (start on desktop, finish on mobile)

---

### Principle 3: Real-Time (Supply Chain Operators)

**Why**: Supply chain is time-sensitive (shipments, inventory, vendor communication)

**Design**:
- Real-time alerts (push notifications)
- Fast response time (sub-second)
- Action-oriented (not just information — "Reroute shipment" button)

---

### Principle 4: Integrated (Corporate Functions)

**Why**: Corporate employees use many tools (Workday, ServiceNow, Excel, email)

**Design**:
- Integrate AI into existing tools (not a separate app)
- Single sign-on (SSO)
- Contextual (AI knows what you're working on)

---

### Principle 5: Simple Language (All Personas)

**Why**: Not everyone is technical

**Design**:
- No jargon ("AI model" → "smart assistant")
- Plain English ("forecast accuracy" → "how often we get it right")
- Visual (charts, graphs, not just numbers)
