## 2. ROI CALCULATION METHODOLOGY (3-BUCKET MODEL)

### Bucket 1: Cost Reduction (40% of ROI)

**Definition**: Direct operational cost savings

**Year 1 Examples**:
- Inventory waste reduction (Old Navy): $2M-$3M
- Markdown waste reduction (Banana Republic): $1.75M-$3.5M
- Labor reallocation (Gap store associates): $400K

**Year 1 Total**: $4.15M-$6.9M

**Year 2 Examples** (scaled):
- Inventory waste reduction (all 4 brands): $8M-$12M
- Markdown waste reduction (all 4 brands): $7M-$14M
- Labor reallocation (all brands): $2M-$3M
- Supply chain efficiency: $3M-$5M

**Year 2 Total**: $20M-$34M

**Year 3 Examples** (optimized):
- Tool consolidation savings: $200K-$300K (reduced platform costs)
- Maintenance reduction: $100K-$200K (one agentic system vs. 100 tools)
- Cumulative from Year 1-2: $20M-$34M (sustained)

**Year 3 Total**: $20.3M-$34.5M

---

### Bucket 2: Productivity Gains (35% of ROI)

**Definition**: Time freed up for higher-value work, faster decision-making, error reduction

**Year 1 Examples**:
- Store associates time with customers: 20% improvement = $500K-$1M (pilot scale)
- Merchandiser time-to-decision: 20-30% reduction = $300K-$500K
- Store manager time on admin: 20% reduction = $200K-$300K

**Year 1 Total**: $1M-$1.8M

**Year 2 Examples** (scaled):
- Store associates time with customers: $2M-$4M (all brands)
- Merchandiser time-to-decision: $1.5M-$2.5M (all brands)
- Store manager time on admin: $1M-$1.5M (all brands)
- Corporate functions (HR, Finance, Legal): $2M-$3M

**Year 2 Total**: $6.5M-$11M

**Year 3 Examples** (optimized):
- Sustained productivity gains: $6.5M-$11M
- Compounding effect (employees get better at using AI): +10-15% = $7M-$12.5M

**Year 3 Total**: $7M-$12.5M

---

### Bucket 3: Cost Avoidance (25% of ROI)

**Definition**: Risk reduction, incidents prevented, compliance violations avoided

**Year 1 Examples**:
- Brand safety incidents prevented: $0 (no incidents = success)
- Compliance violations avoided: $0 (no violations = success)
- Employee turnover reduction: 10-15% = $500K-$1M (pilot scale)

**Year 1 Total**: $500K-$1M

**Year 2 Examples** (scaled):
- Employee turnover reduction: $2M-$4M (all brands)
- Stockout prevention (lost sales recovery): $3M-$5M
- Overstock prevention (markdown avoidance): $2M-$3M

**Year 2 Total**: $7M-$12M

**Year 3 Examples** (optimized):
- Sustained cost avoidance: $7M-$12M

**Year 3 Total**: $7.5M-$13M

## 3. BENCHMARK DATA (RETAIL COMPARABLES)

### Walmart ($500M AI Investment, 2025)

**Results**:
- 30% logistics cost savings
- 15% waste reduction (perishable goods)
- 20% employee productivity boost
- 18-week reduction in fashion production timelines

**Gap Comparison**:
- Walmart: $500M investment (centralized, big bet)
- Gap: $7.2M-$7.8M investment (phased, FDE model)
- Walmart: 30% logistics savings → Gap: 20-30% inventory/supply chain savings (similar)
- Walmart: 20% productivity → Gap: 20-30% productivity (similar or better)

**Conclusion**: Gap can achieve similar results at 1/65th the cost (FDE model vs. platform build)

---

### Zara (Demand Forecasting at Scale)

**Results**:
- 85% sell-through at full price (vs. 70-75% industry average)
- Restocking in days (vs. weeks for competitors)
- Reduced overproduction, stockouts, markdowns

**Gap Comparison**:
- Zara: 85% sell-through → Gap: 70-75% → 85-90% (Pilot 1 target)
- Zara: Days to restock → Gap: Weeks → Days (supply chain AI, Year 2)

**Conclusion**: Gap can match Zara's performance with AI-powered demand forecasting

---

### Perry Ellis (Operations Automation)

**Results**:

**Gap Comparison**:
- Perry Ellis: 95% SKU launch time reduction → Gap: 30-40% (product design acceleration, Year 2)
- Perry Ellis: 25% conversion increase → Gap: 10-15% (customer experience AI, Year 2-3)

**Conclusion**: Gap can achieve similar operational efficiency gains

---

### LVMH (Maison Autonomy Model)

**Results**:
- 40K+ employees using MaIA (generative AI agent)

- Improved conversions (specific % not disclosed)

**Gap Comparison**:

- LVMH: Maison autonomy (75 brands) → Gap: 4 brands (simpler governance)

**Conclusion**: Gap can replicate LVMH's maison autonomy model at smaller scale

## 5. FAILURE COST (WHAT IF WE DON'T DO THIS?)

### Competitive Risk

**Walmart**: Already deployed system-centric agentic AI (Sparky, My Assistant, Marty), $500M investment
**Target**: Deploying AI for forecasting, inventory, store operations
**LVMH**: 40K employees using MaIA, 2M+ monthly requests
**Zara**: 85% sell-through at full price (AI demand forecasting)

**Gap**: Early in AI journey (AI innovation unit established 2024, Google Cloud partnership October 2025)

**If Gap doesn't act**: Falls behind competitors, loses market share, margins erode

**Cost of inaction**: $50M-$100M/year in lost efficiency (inventory waste, markdown waste, productivity)

---

### Talent Risk

**AI talent is scarce**: 77% of AI jobs require master's degrees, 58% of FDE jobs at growth-stage startups

**If Gap doesn't build Office of AI**: Can't attract/retain AI talent, falls behind in AI maturity

**Cost of inaction**: $10M-$20M/year in lost productivity (employees leave for AI-native companies)

---

### Strategic Risk

**CEO Richard Dickson's priority**: "Digital-first organization and mindset"

**If Gap doesn't execute AI strategy**: Fails to deliver on CEO vision, loses executive confidence

**Cost of inaction**: Strategic misalignment, organizational drift

---

### Total Failure Cost: $60M-$120M/year

**Comparison to Investment**: $1.6M-$1.7M Year 1 investment vs. $60M-$120M/year failure cost

**Risk-adjusted decision**: Investing $1.6M to avoid $60M-$120M/year loss is a no-brainer
