## PART 4: GAME THEORY IMAGE SELECTION

### Nash Equilibrium Analysis

**Scenario**: Each image "competes" for customer attention and budget. Which combination maximizes total revenue?

**Game Setup**:
- **Players**: 4 images (RSFM, Green Wildstyle, MAN Tag, Pink Victorian)
- **Strategies**: Each image can be "hero" (large edition) or "support" (small edition)
- **Payoff**: Revenue minus cannibalization cost

### Payoff Matrix (Simplified)

| Strategy | RSFM Hero | Green Hero | MAN Hero | Pink Hero |
|----------|-----------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **RSFM Hero** | $7,800 | -$1,200 | -$800 | -$400 |
| **Green Hero** | -$1,200 | $4,800 | -$600 | -$300 |
| **MAN Hero** | -$800 | -$600 | $4,125 | -$200 |
| **Pink Hero** | -$400 | -$300 | -$200 | $4,500 |

**Interpretation**:
- RSFM as hero generates $7,800 but cannibalizes $1,200 from Green (customers buy RSFM instead of Green)
- Green as hero generates $4,800 but cannibalizes $1,200 from RSFM
- MAN and Pink have lower cannibalization (different aesthetics)

### Nash Equilibrium Solution

**Optimal Strategy**: 
1. **RSFM = Hero** (150 units, multiple products)
2. **Green Wildstyle = Secondary** (100 units, 1-2 products)
3. **MAN Tag = Tertiary** (75 units, 1 product)
4. **Pink Victorian = Accent** (100 units, niche products like hats/totes)

**Why**: RSFM has highest individual payoff AND lowest cannibalization risk (it's a logo, not competing with art pieces).
