# Argy Research: MQ Credibility Assessment
**Agent**: Argentavis (Argy) | **Phase**: 1 — Research | **Date**: 2026-02-11

---

## Executive Summary

MQ (also known as MKUE, MQUE, MQism) is a **well-documented, legendary NYC graffiti writer** who began writing in the **early 1980s** in Manhattan and Queens. He is affiliated with **DMS (Doc Marten Stomp)** crew, deeply connected to NYC hardcore punk culture. MQ is NOT an obscure figure — he has gallery representation, published interviews, museum documentation, and a dedicated Living Proof New York profile calling him a "New York Graffiti Icon."

**Critical finding**: The claim that MQ "painted alongside Keith Haring in the 1970s" requires refinement. MQ started in the early 1980s (not 1970s), and while he and Haring were active in the same NYC scene during the same era, a direct painting-alongside relationship is **unverified**. The narrative should be reframed to emphasize MQ's documented 1980s NYC credentials and his verified, ongoing connection to Tie One.

## 2. The Keith Haring Connection

### What's Verified
- Keith Haring arrived in NYC in 1978, became active in the graffiti/street art scene from 1980 onward
- MQ began writing in the early 1980s in Manhattan
- They were **active in the same city, during the same era, in overlapping creative communities**
- Haring's documented collaborators: Angel "LA II" Ortiz (primary), Jean-Michel Basquiat, Kenny Scharf
- The NYC scene was small enough that overlap is highly plausible

### What's NOT Verified
- A direct "painted alongside" relationship between MQ and Haring
- MQ appears to have started in the early 1980s, not the 1970s
- Haring's documented collaborator circle doesn't explicitly include MQ

### Assessment
The Keith Haring claim is **plausible but unverifiable through public sources**. The 1980s NYC art/graffiti scene was a small, interconnected world. However, "painted alongside" implies direct collaboration, which goes beyond being in the same scene.

### Recommended Narrative Framing
Instead of: *"MQ painted alongside Keith Haring in the 1970s"*
Use: *"MQ came up in the same early 1980s Manhattan graffiti scene that produced Keith Haring, Basquiat, and the entire downtown art revolution"*

This is:
- ✅ Verifiable (MQ was in Manhattan early 1980s, same scene)
- ✅ Impressive (connects MQ to the most valuable art movement of the 20th century)
- ✅ Not dependent on a specific claim about Haring
- ✅ Still positions MQ as a legend from the golden era

If MQ can provide specific verification (photos, witness testimony, documentary footage), the claim can be strengthened.

### Assessment
The MQ → Tie One connection is **well-documented and ongoing**. This is not a new or opportunistic tribute — MQ has been creating Tie One memorial art for **25+ years** (since at least 2000). The shared crew references (DMS, BBB) suggest a real community connection, not just symbolic tribute.

**This is the strongest narrative element**: An artist who has been honoring his fallen friend for a quarter century, now creating a definitive tribute collection.
