## PRIORITY 1: THE REVENUE MATH (Critical — Week 1-2)

### Problem
The underwriter correctly identifies the unit economics don't hold. $500K retail from
one gallery requires $1,923/day across 5 open days = 10 transactions/day at $200 avg.
After 50/50 split and COGS, Rossi retains only $42.5K from retail — forcing the other
6 streams to carry $600K+ in costs. Year 1 expenses ($855K) exceed revenue ($780K)
by $75K *before* any stress scenario.

### Integrated Suggestions

1. **BASELINE DATA PACKAGE** (Underwriter Priority 1 — non-negotiable)
   - Pull trailing 12-month revenue from existing Shopify/POS system
   - Document: monthly transactions, AOV, top 10 artists by revenue, foot traffic
   - If current annual revenue is ~$350-400K, the $500K retail target is a credible
     20-40% growth story. If it's $150K, the entire projection must be rebuilt
   - Template: Monthly Revenue | Transaction Count | AOV | Unique Customers

2. **RECALIBRATE YEAR 1 TO THREE SCENARIOS**
   - Bull case: $780K (current projections — retain as aspirational)
   - Base case: $550-600K (15-20% growth over current + new streams ramping)
   - Bear case: $420-480K (flat retail + only 50% of new streams materializing)
   - For each: show which costs get cut and in what order
   - Critical: the $150K funding request must cover the bear case deficit (~$137K gap)
     with 3-month operating reserve ($35-40K). Total ask should be $185-200K

3. **MONTHLY CASH FLOW MODEL — MONTHS 1-18** (Underwriter Priority 2)
   - Grants don't arrive on schedule. SFAC typically pays quarterly, 60-90 days after
     award notification. California Arts Council has 4-6 month processing times
   - Workshops are seasonal (higher Sept-May, dead June-Aug)
   - Events have startup costs 30-60 days before revenue
   - Model must show: Can Rossi survive Months 1-6 before first grant check arrives?
   - Include: minimum cash threshold ($15-20K) and emergency trigger actions

4. **CREATIVE GROWTH AS TRUE FINANCIAL COMPARABLE**
   - Creative Growth Art Center (Oakland) is the closest validated model:
     nonprofit gallery, 140 artists, 50/50 split, art sales exceeding $1M/year,
     SFMOMA partnership, 50+ year track record
   - Key difference: Creative Growth serves artists with disabilities (stronger
     grant eligibility) but the economic structure is nearly identical to Rossi
   - Request their public Form 990 (available on ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer)
     to benchmark: revenue mix, overhead ratio, program expenses, growth trajectory
   - Use this as anchor point instead of streetwear brand analogies

## PRIORITY 2: PARTNER GALLERY ECONOMICS (Critical — Week 2-3)

### Problem
The underwriter nails it: partners pay no franchise fees, Rossi only earns 20% finder's
fee on collaborations it brokers. So the $470K Year 2 revenue increase is largely phantom
— if partners keep their own revenue, Rossi's actual uplift from adding partners is
maybe $30-50K in brokered fees.

### Integrated Suggestions

5. **REDESIGN THE PARTNER VALUE PROPOSITION**
   Game theory test — what does the partner's Nash equilibrium look like?

   Partner's options:
   A) Join Rossi network (cost: cultural alignment requirements, SKU caps, profit-split compliance)
   B) Stay independent (cost: no shared content, no brand recognition, no collaboration pipeline)
   C) Join then leave (cost: reputation risk, lose network access)

   For (A) to be dominant strategy, Rossi must offer value partners can't get alone:
   - Shared content production (Rossi documents their artists too — $30-60K value)
   - Collaboration pipeline access (brand deals brokered through Rossi network)
   - Traveling exhibition circuit (artists rotate between partner galleries)
   - Shared back-office: Shopify setup, inventory management, fiscal sponsorship access
   - Cross-promotion: each partner gallery promotes the full network

   Quantify: "Joining the Rossi network adds $X in annual value to a partner gallery
   through shared services, content, and collaboration access vs. $0 franchise fee"

6. **REVENUE FLOW ARCHITECTURE**
   Currently unclear how money moves. Design explicit flows:
   - Rossi flagship → Rossi keeps retail margin per 50/50 model
   - Partner gallery retail → Partner keeps their margin, Rossi gets 0% (correct?)
   - Brokered collaboration → Rossi takes 15-20% finder's fee
   - Traveling exhibition → Revenue split TBD (this is the missing piece)
   - Shared merch/online store → Rossi takes platform fee (5-10% of partner sales?)

   The missing revenue lever: if Rossi operates the shared e-commerce platform and
   takes a 10% platform fee on partner online sales, that creates scalable network revenue
   without franchise fees. This is the Etsy/Shopify model applied to gallery cooperatives.

7. **LETTERS OF INTENT FROM 2-3 POTENTIAL PARTNERS** (Underwriter Priority 3)
   - Oakland is the natural first partner (Creative Growth adjacency, BART accessible)
   - LA Arts District has 3-4 graffiti galleries that could be approached
   - Even informal "we would consider joining" letters dramatically improve fundability

## PRIORITY 3: PIPELINE VALIDATION (High — Week 3-4)

### Problem
The underwriter identifies the four weak evidence claims as "the entire thesis of the
pipeline model." If workshop-to-roster conversion is 3% instead of 10-15%, the pipeline
produces 1/3 the artists. If corporate workshops command $1,200 instead of $5,000,
the Robin Hood subsidy model collapses.

### Integrated Suggestions

8. **DOCUMENT 3-5 ARTIST CASE STUDIES NOW**
   Rossi already represents 80 artists. Some version of the pipeline has already happened
   informally. Interview 3-5 artists and document:
   - How they found Rossi
   - What Rossi provided (wall space, events, introductions, documentation)
   - What they earned through Rossi last 12 months
   - What their trajectory looked like before vs. after
   - Would they recommend Rossi to other artists? (Net Promoter Score)

   These don't need to be "workshop → residency → career launch" stories yet.
   They need to show: "Rossi representation materially improved my art career."

9. **RUN ONE PILOT WORKSHOP BEFORE SUBMITTING**
   - Host a single paid workshop ($50-100 sliding scale) in March 2026
   - Document: attendance, demographics, satisfaction, "would you come back?"
   - Track: did any attendee subsequently engage with Rossi (visit gallery, buy art,
     apply for residency)?
   - Even one data point ("our pilot workshop had 18 attendees at $75 avg, 4 expressed
     interest in ongoing engagement") transforms the pipeline from theory to evidence

10. **BENCHMARK PIPELINE RATES AGAINST CREATIVE GROWTH & MEOW WOLF**
    - Creative Growth: 140 artists, $1M+ annual art sales, 50/50 split, artists acquired
      by MoMA, SFMOMA, Centre Pompidou. They've run this model for 50 years.
      What's their artist intake rate? How many artists per year join vs. leave?
    - Meow Wolf: Started as 7 artists pulling a name from a hat. Now $150M+ revenue,
      1,200+ jobs, 5 permanent locations. Their "inner circle" enters an "Artist Bonus
      Pool" for profit sharing. What was their early conversion/retention rate?
    - Fractured Atlas: Fiscal sponsor for 3,500+ artistic projects. They have aggregate
      data on fundraising success rates for sponsored artists — request benchmarks

11. **ADDRESS THE PARETO DISTRIBUTION HONESTLY**
    The underwriter correctly flags: 10-15 artists likely generate 80% of sales.
    Bottom 65 artists earn $200-800/year. The plan should:
    - Acknowledge this distribution explicitly
    - Explain the pipeline's role: move artists UP the revenue curve over time
    - Define "roster tiers": Featured Artists (top 15, gallery wall space),
      Emerging Artists (middle 30, events + online), Discovery Artists (bottom 35,
      workshops + community)
    - Show how an artist progresses from Discovery → Emerging → Featured
    - This IS the Chito model (streets → internet → Supreme → Givenchy) applied
      at organizational scale

## PRIORITY 4: GOVERNANCE SIMPLIFICATION (Medium — Week 4)

### Problem
A dual-board with 14-17 decision-makers for a 2.25 FTE organization doing $780K
revenue is over-governed. The underwriter recommends scaling governance WITH the org.

### Integrated Suggestions

12. **PHASE GOVERNANCE IN THREE STAGES**
    - Year 1 (< $500K): Single 5-7 member advisory board
      * 2-3 artists from current roster
      * 1 nonprofit expert (ideally someone from Creative Growth or Fractured Atlas network)
      * 1 community representative (Mission District resident)
      * 1 financial/legal advisor
    - Year 2 ($500K-$1M, 2+ locations): Add artist advisory council (3-5 artists)
      * Artists gain formal veto on brand collaborations
      * Advisory board expands to 7-9 members
    - Year 3+ ($1M+, 5+ locations): Full dual-board structure as currently designed

    This preserves the artist governance commitment while being realistic about
    operational capacity at each stage.
