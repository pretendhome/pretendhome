**Comprehensive Mapping of Taggers, Online Sellers & Physical Locations**

Generated: 2026-02-07  
Purpose: Map the complete ecosystem of graffiti artist merchandise sales (online + physical)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This research identifies **50+ graffiti artists** with active merchandise operations across:
- **Online platforms**: Etsy, Instagram shops, dedicated websites, StockX, Redbubble
- **Physical locations**: NYC, LA, London, Oakland, Portland, Seattle
- **Sales channels**: Direct-to-consumer, gallery representation, print editions, streetwear collaborations

**Key Finding**: Most established taggers operate **hybrid models** (online shop + gallery representation + brand collaborations), validating Rossi's multi-channel approach.

## ONLINE PLATFORMS (Multi-Artist Marketplaces)

### Primary Platforms

1. **GraffitiStreet.com**
   - Artists: Banksy, Invader, Os Gemeos, Spidertag, and 100+ others
   - Model: Gallery representation + online sales
   - Commission: Likely 40-50%
   - Ships worldwide

2. **Etsy**
   - 10,000+ graffiti-related listings
   - Mix of original artists + unauthorized reproductions
   - Commission: 6.5% transaction fee + 3% payment processing
   - Categories: Prints, digital downloads, custom commissions, streetwear

3. **Redbubble**
   - Print-on-demand for graffiti designs
   - Artists set markup (typically 20-30%)
   - Redbubble handles production + shipping
   - Products: T-shirts, stickers, phone cases, posters

4. **StockX**
   - Authenticated prints and collectibles
   - Artists: Futura 2000, Cope2, KAWS
   - Model: Auction/marketplace (9.5% seller fee)
   - Verification service for authenticity

5. **DirtyPilot.com**
   - Curated graffiti artist prints
   - Artists: Cope2, Lady Pink, and others
   - Model: Gallery representation + online sales
   - Limited edition prints with certificates

6. **Sprayed Paint Art Collection**
   - Artists: Retna, Mear One, Os Gemeos, Mac
   - Model: Online gallery
   - Ships worldwide

7. **The Street Art Store (TheStreetArtStore.com)**
   - Original street art designs on clothing + accessories
   - Model: Direct-to-consumer streetwear
   - Focus: Apparel over prints

## REVENUE MODELS OBSERVED

### 1. Direct-to-Consumer (D2C)
- **Examples**: Cope2-Merch.com, SaberOne.com, LadyPinkNYC.com
- **Margins**: 70-90% (after production costs)
- **Platforms**: Shopify, Big Cartel, WooCommerce
- **Fulfillment**: Self-managed or third-party (Printful, Printify)

### 2. Gallery Representation
- **Examples**: Retna, Mear One, Os Gemeos
- **Commission**: 40-50% to gallery
- **Artist keeps**: 50-60%
- **Benefits**: Curation, authentication, collector network

### 3. Print Editions
- **Examples**: DirtyPilot, Edition Copenhagen, GraffitiStreet
- **Model**: Limited editions (50-500 prints)
- **Price**: $200-$10K depending on artist
- **Split**: 50/50 artist/publisher

### 4. Marketplace Platforms
- **Examples**: Etsy, Redbubble, StockX
- **Fees**: 6.5-20% platform fees
- **Artist keeps**: 80-93.5%
- **Volume**: High volume, lower margins

### 5. Brand Collaborations
- **Examples**: KAWS x Uniqlo, Futura x Kenzo
- **Model**: Licensing + royalties
- **Upfront**: $10K-$500K
- **Royalties**: 5-15% of wholesale

### 6. Artist Collectives (No-Commission)
- **Examples**: Side Rail Collective, Alberta Street Gallery
- **Model**: Membership fees ($50-$200/month)
- **Artist keeps**: 100% of sales (Side Rail) or 70% (Alberta Street)
- **Trade-off**: Artists work gallery shifts

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR ROSSI

### 1. Hybrid Models Dominate
- Most successful artists use **3-5 channels simultaneously**:
  - Direct website (D2C)
  - Instagram shop
  - Gallery representation
  - Brand collaborations
  - Print editions

### 2. Instagram is Primary Discovery Channel
- Artists with 100K+ followers can drive direct sales
- "Link in bio" strategy is standard
- Limited drops create urgency

### 3. Physical Locations Still Matter
- NYC, LA, Miami, London have highest concentration
- Oakland has active artist collective scene (Gray Loft, Art Murmur)
- Portland/Seattle have cooperative models (Alberta Street, Side Rail)

### 4. Commission Rates Vary Widely
- **Traditional galleries**: 40-50%
- **Online platforms**: 6.5-20%
- **Artist collectives**: 0-30%
- **Brand collabs**: 5-15% royalties

### 5. Authentication is Critical
- StockX, DirtyPilot, Edition Copenhagen provide certificates
- Etsy has massive unauthorized reproduction problem
- Artists need authentication strategy

### 6. Price Ranges by Tier
- **Tier 1 (Banksy, KAWS)**: Prints $5K-$50K, originals $500K-$25M
- **Tier 2 (Cope2, Lady Pink)**: Prints $200-$2K, originals $5K-$50K
- **Tier 3 (Emerging)**: Prints $50-$500, originals $500-$5K

### 7. Rossi's Model is Validated
- **Partner gallery network**: Alberta Street, Side Rail prove cooperative models work
- **Online + physical**: All successful artists use hybrid approach
- **Artist-friendly economics**: 70/30 splits exist (Alberta Street)
- **No-commission possible**: Side Rail Collective charges membership fees instead

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Direct Competitors to Rossi

1. **GraffitiStreet.com**
   - Strength: 100+ established artists, global shipping
   - Weakness: Traditional 40-50% commission

2. **DirtyPilot.com**
   - Strength: Curated selection, authentication
   - Weakness: Limited to prints, no physical location

3. **Artist Collectives (Alberta Street, Side Rail)**
   - Strength: Artist-friendly economics (70/30 or 100%)
   - Weakness: Local only, no online scale

4. **Etsy**
   - Strength: Massive reach, low fees (6.5%)
   - Weakness: No curation, authentication issues, race to bottom on pricing

### Rossi's Competitive Advantage

1. **Better economics than galleries** (70/30 or 80/20 vs 50/50)
2. **Authentication + curation** (vs Etsy's free-for-all)
3. **Hybrid model** (online + partner galleries vs online-only)
4. **Artist development** (workshops, mentorship vs transactional platforms)
5. **Community focus** (vs purely commercial galleries)
