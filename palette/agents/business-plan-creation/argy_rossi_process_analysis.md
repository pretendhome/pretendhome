## 6. Key Insights for Replication

### What's Essential

**Comparable Organization Anchoring**:
- Find 1-2 organizations solving same problem at scale
- Get their financial data (Form 990 for nonprofits, public filings for companies)
- Use as validation for customer's model
- **Why essential**: Proves model works, not just theory

**Evidence-Based Claims**:
- Every claim must cite source
- Use [Research: ...] and [Case Study Pattern: ...] markers
- No assertions without backing
- **Why essential**: Builds credibility, enables validation

**ONE-WAY DOOR Decision Framework**:
- Identify 3-5 critical decisions that are hard to reverse
- Flag with ðŸš¨ and require human confirmation
- Provide gradient descent options (3 choices per decision)
- **Why essential**: Prevents silent commitments, forces alignment

**Financial Modeling**:
- 3-year revenue projections
- Cash flow analysis (identifies crises)
- Funding requirements with reserve buffer
- **Why essential**: Uncovers non-obvious problems (Month 17 crisis)

### What's Customizable

**Industry Focus**:
- Rossi: Graffiti/street art (nonprofit)
- Could be: SaaS, retail, service, food/beverage
- **How to adapt**: Change comparable organizations, market research focus

**Document Length**:
- Rossi: 25-page report + 80 pages supporting docs
- Could be: 10-page brief for simple business, 50+ pages for complex
- **How to adapt**: Scale based on complexity and stakeholder needs

**Revenue Model**:
- Rossi: 7 revenue streams (retail, workshops, grants, etc.)
- Could be: Single revenue stream (SaaS subscription) or 10+ streams (marketplace)
- **How to adapt**: Research comparable organizations' revenue mix

**Timeline**:
- Rossi: 1-2 weeks (with async customer input)
- Could be: 3-5 days (urgent) or 4-6 weeks (comprehensive)
- **How to adapt**: Adjust research depth and iteration cycles

### What's Automatable

**Market Research**:
- Comparable organization search (Perplexity, web search)
- Form 990 data extraction (IRS database)
- Market sizing (industry reports, government data)
- **How agent does it**: Argy with Perplexity API

**Financial Modeling**:
- Revenue projections (based on comparable data)

- **How agent does it**: Rex with financial templates

**Document Assembly**:
- Combine research + strategy into narrative
- Apply evidence markers [Research: ...] [Case Study: ...]
- Format with consistent structure
- **How agent does it**: Yuty with templates

**Quality Validation**:
- Check for unsourced claims
- Identify missing data
- Score completeness
- **How agent does it**: Anky with validation checklist

### What Requires Human Input

**Strategic Decisions**:
- Revenue model selection (ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOOR)

## 7. Recommended Process Template

### Phase 1: Discovery (1-2 hours)
**Agent**: Human + Orchestrator

**Steps**:
1. Customer interview (structured questions)
   - Business description, stage, industry
   - Current challenges and pain points
   - Goals (short-term + long-term)
   - Target customers
   - Current operations and resources
   - Constraints and non-negotiables

2. Create convergence brief (RIU-001)
   - Goal: What success looks like
   - Roles: Who does what (agents + human)
   - Constraints: What cannot change
   - Non-goals: What's out of scope
   - Success criteria: How we know we're done

3. Scope and timeline agreement (ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOOR)
   - Document length and depth
   - Timeline (1-2 weeks typical)
   - Customer time required (2-4 hours)

**Outputs**: convergence_brief.md

### Phase 2: Research (2-4 hours)
**Agent**: Argentavis (Argy)

**Steps**:
1. Comparable organization search
   - Find 2-3 organizations solving same problem at scale
   - Get financial data (Form 990, public filings, reports)
   - Extract key metrics (revenue, customers, model)

2. Market research
   - Market size and trends (TAM, SAM, SOM)
   - Customer segments and needs
   - Competitive landscape
   - Industry best practices

3. Financial benchmarks
   - Revenue mix (what % from each stream)
   - Cost structure (what % on each expense)
   - Growth rates (typical Year 1-3)
   - Funding requirements (typical raise amounts)

**Outputs**: research_report.md with [Research: ...] citations

### Phase 3: Strategy Development (2-3 hours)
**Agent**: Tyrannosaurus (Rex)

**Steps**:
1. Revenue model design
   - Identify 3-7 revenue streams
   - Benchmark against comparables
   - Project 3-year revenue mix
   - Flag revenue model as ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOOR

2. Organizational structure
   - Legal structure (nonprofit, for-profit, hybrid)
   - Governance model (board size, roles)
   - Team requirements (hires, contractors)

3. Financial modeling
   - 3-year revenue projections
   - Cash flow analysis (identify crises)
   - Funding requirements (with reserve buffer)
   - Flag funding ask as ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOOR

4. Expansion strategy
   - Geographic expansion (if applicable)
   - Product/service expansion
   - Partnership model

**Outputs**: strategic_framework.md with ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOORS flagged

### Phase 4: Document Assembly (2-3 hours)
**Agent**: Yutyrannus (Yuty)

**Steps**:
1. Executive summary (2 pages)
   - Business overview
   - Market opportunity
   - Strategic recommendations
   - Funding requirements
   - Go/no-go recommendation

2. Decision framework (3 pages)
   - 3-5 ONE-WAY DOORS with gradient descent options
   - Human confirmation checkboxes
   - Game theory analysis (when each option wins)

3. Business plan sections (15-20 pages)
   - Market analysis (with [Research: ...] citations)
   - Revenue model (with comparable benchmarks)
   - Organizational structure
   - Financial projections
   - Implementation plan (6-week action plan)

4. Research appendix (2-5 pages)
   - Comparable organization data
   - Market research sources
   - Financial benchmarks

**Outputs**: business_plan_draft.docx or .md

### Phase 5: Quality Validation (1-2 hours)
**Agent**: Ankylosaurus (Anky)

**Steps**:
1. Completeness check
   - All sections present?
   - All claims sourced?
   - All ONE-WAY DOORS flagged?

2. Quality scoring
   - Evidence quality (sourced vs unsourced)
   - Comparable validity (credible vs weak)
   - Financial rigor (modeled vs guessed)
   - Actionability (executable vs aspirational)

3. Gap identification
   - Missing baseline data
   - Missing case studies
   - Inconsistent numbers
   - Unsupported claims

4. Validation report
   - Score (0-100)
   - What's validated
   - What's missing
   - Recommendations

**Outputs**: validation_report.md with score and gaps

### Phase 6: Customer Review & Iteration (1 week async)
**Agent**: Human (customer)

**Steps**:
1. Customer reviews draft
   - Reads executive summary (5 min)
   - Reviews ONE-WAY DOORS (15 min)
   - Scans full document (30 min)

2. Feedback session
   - Clarifying questions
   - Corrections to assumptions
   - Additional context

3. Iteration (if needed)
   - Agents update based on feedback
   - Re-validate with Anky
   - Customer re-reviews

4. Final approval (ðŸš¨ ONE-WAY DOOR)
   - Customer signs off
   - Document goes live

**Outputs**: Final approved business plan
